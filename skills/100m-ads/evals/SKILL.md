# Paid Ad Copy

## Purpose

Write high-converting paid ad copy rooted in the Hormozi offer framework ($100M Offers + GOATed Ads Playbook) and tuned for modern platform AI systems (Meta Andromeda, Google GEM). Always produce complete ad sets — not single ads — with platform-correct field formats, character limits, strategic angles, and brief reasoning for every variation.

## When to Use

- User wants to write or improve Facebook/Instagram, Google, LinkedIn, or TikTok ad copy
- User mentions "ad copy," "headlines," "primary text," "ad creative," "hook," or "CTA"
- User wants to create a campaign from scratch or test new angles
- User asks for help with a specific offer, product, or promotion
- User wants to optimize underperforming ads or scale winners

## Step 1: Ask Clarifying Questions Before Writing

Never write copy without these inputs. Ask all at once in a single message, grouped clearly:

**Required:**
1. **Platform** — Facebook/Instagram, Google Search, Google Performance Max, LinkedIn, TikTok, or multiple?
2. **Product/Offer** — What exactly are you selling or promoting? What's the specific offer (price, deal, guarantee, or lead magnet)?
3. **Dream Outcome** — What specific result does the customer get? (Be precise: "lose 20 lbs in 60 days," "book 10 more sales calls/month")
4. **Target audience** — Who is this for? Demographics, pain points, awareness level (do they know they have a problem? Do they know solutions exist? Have they heard of you?)
5. **Primary CTA goal** — What action should the ad drive? (Purchase, lead form, call, download, free trial, demo)
6. **Cold or warm audience** — Is this targeting cold traffic (never heard of you), warm retargeting (visited site, watched video), or existing customers?

**Optional but improves output significantly:**
- Brand voice or tone (casual/serious/bold/clinical)
- Competitor differentiation — what makes this offer unique vs. alternatives?
- Any winning angles or hooks from past ads
- Any content, testimonials, or proof to draw from

If the user has a `.claude/product-marketing-context.md`, read it first and skip questions already answered.

## Step 2: Diagnose Audience Awareness Level

Before choosing angles and hooks, identify where the audience sits on the Schwartz Awareness Ladder. This determines the entire strategy.

| Level | Who They Are | Hook Strategy | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------------|---------|
| **Most Aware** | Know you, just need a reason to act | Offer-driven — lead with the deal | "50% off our [product] this week only" |
| **Product-Aware** | Know products like yours exist, but not you | Proof-driven — social proof, results, reviews | "10,000 customers lost 20+ lbs with [X]" |
| **Solution-Aware** | Know they want a result but haven't committed | Promise-driven — your unique mechanism | "Lose 15 lbs in 30 days without cutting carbs" |
| **Problem-Aware** | Know they have a problem, no solution in mind | Pain-driven — mirror their frustration | "Tired of diets that don't stick? There's a better way." |
| **Completely Unaware** | Don't know they have a problem | Curiosity-driven — reveal a hidden truth | "The hidden reason 1 in 3 people can't lose weight" |

Cold audiences = skew toward Problem-Aware or Completely Unaware hooks.
Warm retargeting = skew toward Product-Aware or Most Aware hooks.
Broad scaling = spread hooks across all 5 levels.

## Step 3: Apply the Hormozi Offer Framework

Every ad must communicate a "Grand Slam Offer" — one so good the prospect feels stupid saying no. Use the Value Equation as the filter for every line of copy:

**Value = (Dream Outcome × Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

To maximize perceived value in copy:
- **Dream Outcome**: Name the specific result, not the feature. "20 qualified leads/month" not "lead generation software."
- **Likelihood of Achievement**: Add proof signals — testimonials, case studies, number of customers, guarantees
- **Reduce Time Delay**: Emphasize speed — "in 30 days," "in your first week," "same day"
- **Reduce Effort & Sacrifice**: Emphasize ease — "without cold calling," "done for you," "no equipment needed"

**Value Stacking**: Every ad should communicate multiple value layers. Stack the outcome, the speed, the ease, and the risk reversal into your body copy — each line earns its keep.

**Risk Reversal in Copy**: Name the guarantee explicitly. "Or your money back." "We only get paid when you do." "First month free if you don't see results." This removes the #1 objection (fear of wasting money) before it forms.

**Objection Handling**: Anticipate the top 2-3 reasons someone won't click and preemptively dismantle them within the body copy. Turn each obstacle into a proof point or solution statement.

## Step 4: Build the Hook (80% of effort goes here)

If the hook doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters. Write the hook before anything else.

**Hook sources to draw from:**
1. Customer's exact words about their problem (from reviews, testimonials, support tickets)
2. A counterintuitive or surprising fact about the category
3. A bold result or outcome stated specifically ("From $0 to $47k/month in 6 months")
4. A direct call-out to the audience ("If you run a gym and can't scale past $30k/month...")
5. A challenge to conventional wisdom ("Everything you've been told about X is wrong")
6. A pain mirror ("Still doing Y and getting nowhere?")

**Pattern interrupt principle**: The hook must make the thumb stop. It must create an open loop — a question the brain needs to close. Test multiple hook angles against the same body copy.

**Hook angles to rotate through:**
- **Fear**: What they'll lose, miss, or continue suffering if they don't act
- **Aspiration**: The specific future state they want — vivid, tangible, personal
- **Curiosity**: A secret, mechanism, or fact they don't know yet
- **Social proof**: Others already getting the result they want
- **Contrarian**: The conventional wisdom is wrong; here's what actually works

---

## Step 5: Write the Ad Body (The Meat)

The body fulfills the hook's promise. Choose a format based on the offer type and audience temperature:

| Format | Best For | Structure |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Demonstration** | Physical products, software, visible results | Show the before/after or live product experience |
| **Testimonial/UGC** | Any offer with proof | Customer voice → specific result → your offer |
| **Education** | Complex products, skeptical audiences | Teach something useful → position offer as logical next step |
| **Story** | Personal brands, emotional offers | Relatable struggle → turning point → outcome → offer |
| **Faceless/Text-only** | Quick-turn testing, DTC products | Proof screenshots, bullet lists, direct statements |

**Body copy rules:**
- Lead with the prospect's problem, not your product
- Use short sentences and line breaks for mobile readability
- One idea per sentence
- Move from pain → solution → proof → offer → CTA in that order
- Every sentence should earn the next one — cut anything that doesn't advance the reader toward clicking

---

## Step 6: Write the CTA

**Clear beats clever.** Tell them exactly what to do, how to do it, when to do it, what they get, and what happens next.

Formula:
> [Action] [How] [When/Urgency] → [What They Get] → [What Happens Next]

Example: "Click 'Learn More' below → Get instant access to our free 3-day trial → We'll set up your account in 5 minutes."

Always include urgency or scarcity if truthful: limited spots, deadline, bonus expiring, price increasing.

## Platform Output Formats

### Facebook / Meta Ads

Always output all required fields. Flag any field over the limit immediately and provide a trimmed version.

```
PRIMARY TEXT (125 chars recommended / 2200 max):
[Copy here]

HEADLINE (27 chars recommended / 40 max):
[Headline here]

DESCRIPTION (27 chars recommended / 30 max):
[Description here]

CTA BUTTON: [Select from: Shop Now | Learn More | Sign Up | Get Quote | Download | Book Now | Apply Now | Subscribe | Contact Us | Watch More | Send Message | Get Offer]

URL SLUG / UTM: utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=[campaign-name]&utm_content=[ad-variant-id]
```

**Meta Andromeda optimization notes:**
- Meta's Andromeda AI ranks ads for delivery using early engagement signals (thumb-stop rate, 3-second video views, link clicks in first hour). Copy must earn attention in the first 3 words of the Primary Text — these are visible in feed before "See More."
- The Headline is often the most-read element in feed and the only element visible in some placements. It must function as a standalone micro-hook.
- Meta tests your creative across placements automatically. Each field must make sense in isolation — Primary Text, Headline, and Description may appear in any combination or alone.
- Avoid policy-risk language: before/after framing on body/weight, income guarantees, political targeting signals, superlatives without substantiation ("best," "#1" need proof).
- High relevance score (ad quality ranking) is driven by engagement rate relative to the target audience. Write to the specific audience, not to everyone. Relevance beats reach.

### Google Search Ads (Responsive Search Ads)

Always output all required fields. Google automatically tests asset combinations — each headline and description must work standalone and in any combination.

```
HEADLINES (up to 15 available; minimum 3 required; 30 chars max each):
H1: [30 chars max]
H2: [30 chars max]
H3: [30 chars max]
H4: [30 chars max — optional but recommended]
H5: [30 chars max — optional but recommended]

DESCRIPTIONS (up to 4 available; minimum 2 required; 90 chars max each):
D1: [90 chars max]
D2: [90 chars max]

SITELINK EXTENSIONS (optional but strongly recommended):
Sitelink 1: [Headline: 25 chars max] | [Desc line 1: 35 chars max] | [Desc line 2: 35 chars max]
Sitelink 2: [same format]
Sitelink 3: [same format]

CALLOUT EXTENSIONS (25 chars max each; add 4-6):
- [Callout 1]
- [Callout 2]
- [Callout 3]
- [Callout 4]

DISPLAY URL PATH:
yourdomain.com/[path1]/[path2]
```

**Google GEM/AI optimization notes:**
- Google's Generative Experience Model (GEM) assembles ad combinations from your asset pool and tests them for ad strength. Aim for "Excellent" ad strength by providing 15 headlines and 4 descriptions with variety — don't repeat the same idea in multiple assets.
- Include keyword-rich headlines (exact match, phrase match) alongside benefit-led and emotional headlines. Google needs both for relevance and Quality Score.
- Each headline must make sense when paired with any other — avoid headlines that only work in sequence. Write them as independent micro-claims.
- Pin sparingly (pinning headlines to position 1/2/3 reduces Google's ability to optimize). Only pin if legally or brand required.
- Callout extensions are always shown when relevant — use them for proof points, differentiators, and guarantees that don't fit in headlines.

## Required Output Structure

For every request, output a complete ad set — not a single ad. Minimum output per platform:

**3 headline variants** (testing different angles: aspiration, proof, pain/fear)
**2 body copy variants** (testing different formats or angles)
**1-2 CTA variations** (different urgency or action framings)

For each ad variation, include a brief **Strategic Note** formatted as:

```
STRATEGIC NOTE:
- Angle: [What psychological lever this uses — fear / aspiration / curiosity / social proof / contrarian]
- Awareness level targeted: [Most Aware / Product-Aware / Solution-Aware / Problem-Aware / Unaware]
- Platform optimization logic: [Why this asset structure will perform well in Meta Andromeda / Google GEM — e.g., "Headline functions standalone for all placements," "Early hook visible before 'See More'"]
- Objection addressed: [The specific doubt or hesitation this copy handles]
```

Always flag character overages with: `[OVER LIMIT: X chars — trimmed below]` and provide the trimmed version immediately.

## Angle Selection Guide

Use this to choose the right angle for the situation:

| Situation | Best Angle | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Cold traffic, unaware audience | Curiosity or Pain | Must earn attention without assumed context |
| Warm retargeting (visited site) | Social Proof or Offer | They already know you; overcome hesitation |
| Existing customers (upsell) | Aspiration or Loyalty | They trust you; sell the next level |
| High-competition category | Contrarian or Mechanism | Stand out by challenging assumptions |
| Price-sensitive audience | Value Stack + Risk Reversal | Justify the investment with ROI and guarantee |
| High-ticket offers | Social Proof + Specificity | Credibility before the ask |
| Impulse purchases | Scarcity + Simplicity | Remove friction; make action feel low-risk |

## Anti-Patterns

**Don't lead with features.** "Powered by AI" means nothing. "Cut your ad spend in half" is what people buy.

**Don't write generic hooks.** "Are you tired of X?" is overused. Be specific — name the exact frustration with vivid language.

**Don't ignore character limits.** Truncated headlines damage click-through rates and quality scores. Always count. Always trim.

**Don't write one ad.** One ad is a guess. A set of 3 angles is a test. A set of 10 is a system.

**Don't forget the guarantee.** If there's a money-back guarantee, trial period, or performance guarantee — it belongs in the copy. Risk reversal is a conversion multiplier.

**Don't write to everyone.** Ads that try to appeal to all audiences resonate with none. Specific language for a specific person outperforms broad language every time.

**Don't use vague CTAs.** "Click here" tells them nothing. "Click to see your free 14-day trial" tells them what they get.

**Don't skip the strategic note.** Every variation needs a documented angle and rationale. This enables learning across iterations.

## Examples

### Example 1: Cold Facebook Ad — B2B SaaS

**Context**: Marketing agency software, cold audience, offer is 14-day free trial, audience = agency owners doing $30k-$150k/month, no brand awareness

**Awareness level**: Problem-Aware (they know client reporting is painful; they don't know this product)

**Angle**: Pain mirror → mechanism → proof → risk reversal

```
PRIMARY TEXT:
You charge clients $3k/month. Then spend 8 hours building their report.
That's $75/hr of your time gone. Every. Single. Month.

[PRODUCT] pulls live data from 30+ ad platforms and auto-builds client
reports in 4 minutes. No copy-paste. No formatting. No Sunday night panic.

4,200 agencies switched in the last 90 days.
Try it free for 14 days — no credit card.

HEADLINE: Automate Client Reports in 4 Min

DESCRIPTION: 30+ integrations. Free 14-day trial.

CTA BUTTON: Start Free Trial

STRATEGIC NOTE:
- Angle: Pain (lost billable time) → aspiration (reclaim your hours) → proof (4,200 agencies)
- Awareness level: Problem-Aware
- Platform optimization logic: First 3 words "You charge clients" immediately identify the reader, improving thumb-stop rate for Andromeda. Headline functions standalone as a clear promise.
- Objection addressed: "Is this worth the hassle of switching?" — handled by 4-minute setup claim and risk-free trial.
```

### Example 2: Google RSA — Local Service Business

**Context**: Weight loss clinic, search campaign, keywords around "weight loss program near me"

**Awareness level**: Solution-Aware (actively searching, knows solutions exist)

```
HEADLINES:
H1: Lose 20 Lbs in 60 Days        [28 chars]
H2: Medical Weight Loss Program    [30 chars]
H3: Free Consultation This Week    [30 chars]
H4: No Fad Diets. Real Results.    [30 chars]
H5: 500+ Patients. Proven System   [30 chars]

DESCRIPTIONS:
D1: Doctor-supervised program tailored to your body. Most patients lose
    20+ lbs in their first 60 days. Free consult — limited spots.    [89 chars]
D2: Stop guessing. Our medical team builds a custom plan, tracks your
    progress, and adjusts as you go. Book your free consultation today.  [90 chars]

CALLOUTS:
- FDA-Approved Methods        [20 chars]
- No Contracts Required       [22 chars]
- Free Initial Consult        [22 chars]
- Covered by Most Insurance   [26 chars — OVER LIMIT, trim to: Most Insurance Accepted [23 chars]]

STRATEGIC NOTE:
- Angle: Proof + specificity + risk removal (free consult, no contracts)
- Awareness level: Solution-Aware (actively searching for programs)
- Platform optimization logic: Headlines 1-5 span multiple message types (outcome, category, urgency, differentiator, proof) so Google GEM can assemble high-relevance combinations for different intents. No headlines are sequential — each stands alone.
- Objection addressed: "Will this actually work for me?" → handled by "tailored to your body" + "500+ patients."
```
